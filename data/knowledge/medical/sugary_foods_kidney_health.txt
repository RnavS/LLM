Sugary candy such as Skittles does not have a unique kidney-specific effect from a small occasional serving in an otherwise healthy person.

The larger concern is repeated high added-sugar intake over time. Diets high in added sugar can contribute to weight gain, insulin resistance, type 2 diabetes, and high blood pressure. Diabetes and hypertension are major long-term risk factors for kidney disease.

For someone who already has kidney disease, diabetes, or high blood pressure, frequent sugary snacks may be more concerning because they can make overall metabolic control harder.

If a person has kidney symptoms, known kidney disease, swelling, blood in the urine, severe dehydration, or questions about a kidney-safe diet, they should speak with a licensed clinician or renal dietitian.
