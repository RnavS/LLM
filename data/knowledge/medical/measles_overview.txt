Measles is a highly contagious viral illness. Common symptoms usually include a high fever, cough, runny nose, red or watery eyes, and a widespread rash that often starts on the face before spreading downward.

Some people also develop sore throat, tiredness, poor appetite, and small white spots inside the mouth known as Koplik spots.

Because measles can lead to complications such as dehydration, ear infection, pneumonia, or inflammation of the brain, medical evaluation is important if symptoms are severe, worsening, or occur in an infant, pregnant person, or someone with a weakened immune system.

If someone has trouble breathing, confusion, severe dehydration, extreme sleepiness, or rapidly worsening symptoms, they should seek urgent medical care right away.
