Sugary candy such as Gushers, Skittles, and similar fruit snacks is not known for causing a unique direct injury to the intestines in an otherwise healthy person after a small occasional serving.

The more typical short-term issue is digestive upset when a person eats a lot at once. That can include stomach discomfort, bloating, nausea, loose stools, or diarrhea because of the sugar load, food additives, and overall gastrointestinal irritation.

For some people, especially those with sensitive digestion, irritable bowel symptoms, or trouble absorbing certain sugars, large amounts of very sweet candy may trigger more noticeable gut symptoms.

The larger long-term concern is overall high added-sugar intake, not a specific intestine-toxic effect of one candy. Frequent high-sugar intake can worsen metabolic health and make healthy digestion and overall nutrition harder to maintain.

Seek medical care if symptoms are severe, persistent, include blood in the stool, repeated vomiting, severe dehydration, strong abdominal pain, or significant weight loss.
