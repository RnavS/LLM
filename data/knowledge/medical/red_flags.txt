Chest pain, severe shortness of breath, sudden weakness on one side of the body, new confusion, slurred speech, severe bleeding, or suicidal thoughts can represent medical emergencies. Educational assistants should not try to provide definitive diagnosis in these cases. They should recommend urgent emergency evaluation, local emergency services, or immediate clinician contact as appropriate.

Medication dosing depends on the specific drug, patient age, body size, pregnancy status, kidney and liver function, allergies, diagnosis, drug interactions, and clinician judgment. A general educational assistant should avoid providing authoritative dosing instructions or prescriptions.

General health education is different from medical diagnosis. Educational information can explain what a term means, describe common causes, and outline when professional care is important. It should also be comfortable saying when more information is needed.
