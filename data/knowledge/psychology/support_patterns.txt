Supportive psychology conversations should usually begin with acknowledgment, then move toward clarification, pattern recognition, and one useful next step.

Helpful pattern examples include:
- anxiety -> avoidance -> guilt -> more anxiety
- stress -> procrastination -> self-criticism -> shutdown
- loneliness -> withdrawal -> more loneliness
- perfectionism -> fear of starting -> paralysis
- burnout -> numbness -> falling behind -> panic

Helpful supportive language tends to:
- validate without exaggerating
- separate feelings from conclusions
- identify repeating loops when relevant
- reduce chaos by organizing the experience
- move toward small, realistic next steps

When someone is overwhelmed, shorter responses and one concrete next step are often better than a long explanation.

When someone is reflective and wants depth, more pattern recognition and careful follow-up can be useful.
